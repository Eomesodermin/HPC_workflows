NKG7 Ion-Channel Hypothesis — Tier 1: Oligomer Prediction & Pore Test

Project: NKG7 in-silico ion-channel evaluation (proj_d2fe638c3a6f) · Tier: 1 (homo-oligomer prediction C2–C6, three predictors, + reference-HOLE pore re-scan) · Date: 2026-07-14 · Cluster: marvin (Uni Bonn HPC)
Target: human NKG7 (UniProt Q16617, 165 aa) · Predictors: Boltz-2, AlphaFold3 (3.0.1), Chai-1 (0.6.1) · Pore tool: reference HOLE via mdahole2 0.5.0.

Tier 1 verdict: the channel hypothesis is NOT supported. Do not escalate to a channel-centric Tier 2/3.
Across three independent structure predictors and five stoichiometries (C2–C6), no NKG7 homo-oligomer is confidently supported (best interface ipTM = 0.63, Boltz-2 C2, below the ~0.8 confident-interface bar; the three predictors disagree on both the preferred stoichiometry and its confidence). More decisively, the pore test and the confidence are anti-correlated: every model any predictor is even moderately confident about is occluded (no through-pore), while the only models with an open channel-sized pore are the lowest-confidence ones — and those have pore axes of 100+ Å, i.e. splayed, non-membrane-like aggregates rather than a compact transmembrane channel. This is the clean result for a scaffold / auxiliary-modulator role and against NKG7 being a self-contained conducting channel — consistent with the Tier-0 skeptical prior (every structural/evolutionary analog is a non-conducting claudin or Ca_V/TARP-γ auxiliary subunit).

1. What this tier asks

Tier 0 established that NKG7 is a genuine claudin/PMP-22/Ca_V-γ tetraspan but that its monomer is occluded (expected — a lone 4-TM subunit rarely encloses a pore). The channel hypothesis therefore stands or falls on oligomerisation: does NKG7 assemble into a symmetric complex whose subunit interfaces enclose a continuous, hydrated, ion-sized pore through the membrane? Tier 1 answers this in two steps: (i) predict the homo-oligomer for each order C2–C6 with three independent methods and score interface confidence + symmetry; (ii) re-scan every candidate with the reference HOLE program for a through-pore.

5. Interpretation & gate decision

Gate: STOP the channel-centric track. The oligomer test — the decisive experiment the whole campaign was built around — comes back negative on both criteria that matter: NKG7 shows no confidently-predicted symmetric assembly, and no confident assembly encloses a pore. Combined with the Tier-0 evidence (no ion-transport/signalling domain; every analog a non-conducting auxiliary subunit or paracellular claudin; occluded monomer), the weight of computational evidence is now clearly against NKG7 being a self-contained ion channel and for a scaffold / auxiliary-modulator role — the same niche as its Ca_V/TARP-γ and GSG1L relatives, and consistent with its established v-ATPase-inhibition / mTORC1 function. Running membrane MD (Tier 3) on a non-confident, occluded oligomer would not rescue the hypothesis; it would only characterise an assembly the predictors don't believe in.

Recommended next step (optional, reframed): if the biology of interest is NKG7's effect on Ca2+, the productive question is no longer "is NKG7 a pore?" but "what does NKG7 dock onto?" — i.e. predict NKG7 against candidate partner channels/transporters (e.g. ORAI1/STIM1, a Ca_V α subunit, or the v-ATPase/ATP6AP2 it is known to bind) as a hetero-complex. That is a different, better-posed campaign.

6. Methods

  • Prediction: each predictor ran the full C2–C6 homo-oligomer sweep as a single A40 GPU job on marvin. Boltz-2 (MSA server, 3 diffusion samples/order); AlphaFold3 3.0.1 (apptainer; MSA data pipeline run once on the monomer, reused across orders); Chai-1 0.6.1 (cached ColabFold MSA, 5 samples/order, no ESM embeddings — see §2 footnote).
  • Symmetry: chain-centroid radial and angular coefficient of variation (low CV = regular Cn ring). AF3 builds near-perfect rings at all orders (radial CV ≤ 0.011) but assigns them low confidence — symmetric placement by the diffusion prior, not evidence of a real interface.
  • Pore: reference HOLE (mdahole2 0.5.0 / MDAnalysis 2.10.0) along each assembly's symmetry axis; minimum radius with Bondi vdW radii. This replaces the Tier-0 geometric HOLE-equivalent with the reference program, as flagged in the Tier-0 limitations.

7. Limitations

  • Static predicted models without membrane, lipid, or side-chain dynamics; a real channel could in principle gate open from a closed prediction — but that would still require a confident closed assembly to start from, which is absent here.
  • Chai-1 ran without ESM embeddings (node network limit). The confidence conclusion is corroborated by two other predictors; the exact Chai ipTM values may shift slightly with ESM.
  • Homo-oligomer only — this tier does not test hetero-complexes with partner proteins (see §5 recommendation).
  • ipTM/pTM are confidence proxies, not free energies; a low ipTM means "the predictor cannot place a confident interface", not a measured non-binding.
  • All computational; channel function is defined electrophysiologically and requires wet-lab confirmation.

8. Glossary

Homo-oligomer / Cn
An assembly of n identical subunits with n-fold rotational (cyclic) symmetry; C2 = dimer … C6 = hexamer.
ipTM / pTM
Interface / global predicted TM-score (0–1); a model-confidence proxy. ipTM ≥ 0.8 ≈ confident interface; ≥ 0.5 minimally credible.
Boltz-2 / AlphaFold3 / Chai-1
Three independent deep-learning structure predictors; used together for consensus because single-method membrane-oligomer prediction is unreliable.
ESM embeddings
Protein-language-model features that refine Chai-1's per-residue accuracy; optional (the model runs without them).
HOLE / pore radius
Program that traces the minimum-radius pathway along a channel axis; ~1.15 Å is the minimum radius to pass one water, ~1.0 Å a dehydrated Na+.
Pore axis length
Length over which HOLE traced a continuous path; for a real membrane channel this is ~the bilayer thickness (~30–40 Å), not 100+ Å.
Ca_V/TARP-γ / GSG1L
Non-conducting auxiliary subunits that regulate calcium and AMPA channels; NKG7's closest structural and evolutionary relatives.
