NKG7 Ion-Channel Hypothesis — Tier 0 Triage Report

Project: NKG7 in-silico ion-channel evaluation (proj_d2fe638c3a6f) · Tier: 0 (structural / sequence / genomic / transcript / domain triage + monomer pore) · Date: 2026-07-14 · Cluster: marvin (Uni Bonn HPC)
Identifiers: UniProt Q16617 (human, 165 aa) / Q99PA5 (mouse, 165 aa) · gene ENSG00000105374 (chr19q13.4) · RefSeq mRNA NM_005601 · aliases GMP-17, GIG-1.

Tier 0 overall verdict: ESCALATE to Tier 1 (oligomer prediction) — with a strong prior caveat.
All five workstreams independently confirm NKG7 is a bona-fide member of the claudin / PMP-22 / Ca_V-γ (CACNG) tetraspan 4-TM superfamily — the architectural prerequisite for the channel hypothesis. However, every specific structural and evolutionary analog is a non-conducting protein: paracellular claudins and regulatory (auxiliary) Ca_V/TARP γ subunits, not pore-forming channels. NKG7 carries no ion-transport domain, no signalling domain, and its monomer encloses no pore (expected). The decisive test — does an oligomer enclose a conducting pore? — is deferred to Tier 1. The Tier-0 evidence is consistent with the channel hypothesis at the family level but already foreshadows a likely scaffold / auxiliary-modulator role (cf. the known v-ATPase/mTORC1 function).

1. What this tier asks

Before spending GPU on structure prediction and MD, Tier 0 establishes what NKG7 is from every cheap angle: its fold, its closest sequence and structural relatives, its gene/transcript architecture, its domain/motif content, and whether the monomer alone could conduct. NKG7 is a 165-aa four-transmembrane protein of cytotoxic-lymphocyte granule membranes with no documented channel activity. The hypothesis under test is that it may nonetheless be an ion channel (most plausibly Ca2+-permeable), motivated by (i) its reported membership in the claudin/PMP-22 tetraspan superfamily and (ii) the literature analogy between its four TM domains and the Ca_V-γ (CACNG) auxiliary subunit of L-type Ca2+ channels, plus the observation that NKG7 loss reduces Ca2+ influx in CD8 T cells.

The honest counter-hypothesis (tested, not assumed away): the Ca_V-γ subunit NKG7 resembles is regulatory and non-conducting; claudins form pores only paracellularly (between two cells); and NKG7 is known to bind and inhibit the v-ATPase (via ATP6AP2), restraining mTORC1. The family precedent is therefore equally consistent with NKG7 being a channel modulator/scaffold that affects Ca2+ indirectly. Tier 0 was designed to distinguish "NKG7 is itself a pore" from "NKG7 looks like a channel-family protein but has no conduction pathway of its own." Both outcomes are scientifically valuable.

3. Tier 0A — Fold-based structural homology (Foldseek)

Method. foldseek easy-search in TM-align mode (--alignment-type 1) on marvin, querying the NKG7 human and mouse AlphaFold monomers against PDB100 and AFDB/Swiss-Prot. Top hits' matched-chain molecule identities were resolved via the RCSB data API (critical: for multi-subunit channel complexes, the matched chain — not just the entry title — decides whether NKG7 resembles a pore or an auxiliary subunit).

  • NKG7 fold's entire top structural neighborhood (TM-score ~0.87-0.90) is the claudin/PMP-22 + Ca_V/TARP-gamma tetraspan superfamily.
  • Top-20 PDB100 hits (human): 10 claudins (claudin-3/4/9/15/19) + 10 Ca_V/TARP gamma auxiliary subunits (CACNG1/2/3/5, stargazin/TARP; incl. GluA2-gamma chimeras whose MATCHED chain is the gamma subunit).

  • Sequence identity to all hits is low (fident ~0.10-0.19) — similarity is fold-level, consistent with distant superfamily membership, not close homology.
  • Mouse replicate reproduces the human result.

4. Tier 0B — Sequence homology & genomic neighborhood

Method. BLASTp (vs nr), BLASTn (NM_005601 CDS vs nt), tBLASTn (vs translated genomes); Ensembl Compara gene tree for paralogy; EBI phmmer for profile-level remote homology; Ensembl REST for the chr19 locus and flanking genes.

  • BLASTp of Q16617 vs nr (250 hits): ALL 250 are NKG7 orthologs; ZERO human paralogs detectable. Closest ortholog Gorilla/Pan NKG7 95.8% id, 100% cov. No MS4A/CACNG/claudin/PMP22 at BLAST-detectable identity — the CaV-gamma/claudin similarity is fold-level, not primary sequence.
  • BLASTn of NKG7 CDS (NM_005601.4, 498 nt) vs nt (250 hits): human nucleotide SINGLETON — no paralogous locus; all cross-species hits are NKG7 orthologs (Pan/Gorilla mRNA 98.6%). Alias GIG-1 confirmed by 100% match to S69115 (G-CSF-induced gene).
  • tBLASTn (protein vs translated nt, 250 hits): NO human pseudogene and NO unannotated homolog; no channel/tetraspan family member in translated genomes.
  • Ensembl Compara gene tree places NKG7 in the PMP22/EMP/MP20/claudin tetraspan (4-TM) superfamily. 10 human paralogs: TMEM202, CLDND2, LIM2/MP20, GSG1L, GSG1, GSG1L2, EMP1, EMP2, EMP3, PMP22 — all at 8–24% identity (below BLAST floor, hence BLAST missed them).
  • GSG1L (an NKG7 paralog) is a claudin-fold AUXILIARY subunit of AMPA-type glutamate-receptor ion channels — same architectural niche as the cited CACNG/CaV-gamma (also claudin-superfamily channel-auxiliary). NKG7's closest functionally-characterised relatives are NON-conducting regulatory channel subunits, not pore-forming channels.
  • EBI HMMER phmmer vs UniProtKB (100 hits, 98 NKG7 orthologs): top hit is a 'Claudin domain-containing protein' (A0A8J6BAY0, E=1.7e-118, 4-TM) scoring above human NKG7 itself — profile-level remote homology BLAST missed, independently confirming claudin-domain 4-TM placement. (The second non-NKG7 hit, A0A821SV16, is an unannotated 'hypothetical protein', not itself claudin-labelled.)
  • NKG7 locus: chr19:51,371,606–51,375,628 (GRCh38, minus strand), within the chr19q13.4 SIGLEC/CD33 immune-gene cluster.
  • NKG7 is physically SANDWICHED between two of its own tetraspan paralogs (NKG7 is minus-strand, so higher coord = 5', lower coord = 3'): LIM2/MP20 on the 5' side (4.3 kb, higher coordinate, lens tetraspan) and CLDND2 on the 3' side (2.6 kb, lower coordinate, claudin-domain-containing) — a local tandem cluster of the claudin/PMP22 superfamily.
  • No voltage-gated or ligand-gated pore-forming ion-channel gene is an immediate genomic neighbor.
  • Strict 1:1 orthology (100 Ensembl orthologues, no lineage-specific NKG7 duplication) — NKG7 is a single-copy, conserved claudin-superfamily tetraspan.
The key evolutionary result: NKG7 is a sequence singleton (no human paralog or pseudogene detectable by BLAST), but Compara + phmmer place it firmly in the claudin/PMP-22 superfamily. Its closest human paralogs (all <25% identity) include GSG1L — a claudin-fold auxiliary subunit of AMPA glutamate-receptor channels — the same non-conducting niche as the cited CACNG/Ca_V-γ. NKG7 sits in a chr19q13.4 tandem cluster of its own tetraspan paralogs (LIM2/MP20 and CLDND2 immediately flanking).

Method. Ensembl REST isoform catalog (ENSG00000105374) cross-referenced to RefSeq and UniProt; each coding isoform mapped onto the 4-TM topology; GTEx v8 gene- and transcript-level expression across 54 tissues.

Method. InterProScan 5 (EBI) on Q16617 — Pfam, PROSITE, SMART, CDD, PANTHER, TMHMM, SignalP, Phobius — plus targeted motif checks and TM-topology confirmation. All family accessions pulled live from the InterProScan result (never from memory).

  • InterProScan 5 (EBI, job iprscan5-R20260714-092415-0953-39633323-p2m) on full 165-aa Q16617 sequence.
  • Channel-family assignment CONFIRMED with exact live accessions: Pfam PF00822 (PMP22_Claudin, IPR004031, res 11-149, E=8.7e-7); PROSITE PS01221 (PMP22_1, IPR004032, res 18-46); PANTHER PTHR10671 (IPR050579, res 2-154, E=2.8e-26); CATH claudin-like fold G3DSA:1.20.140.150.   • NO signalling or binding domain detected: no kinase/phosphatase, SH2/SH3, PDZ, ITAM/ITIM, adaptor, or ion-transport (Pfam Ion_trans/channel) domain. The only GO term on PF00822 is GO:0016020 (membrane) — no channel/transport GO term.
  • 4-TM topology CONFIRMED by TMHMM: TM1 7-29, TM2 63-85, TM3 92-114, TM4 129-151 — matches assumed 9-29/61-81/92-112/133-153 within +/-2-4 residues.
  • Orientation (Phobius): cytoplasmic N-tail (1-6), ECL1 non-cyto 38-60, short cyto loop 86-91, ECL2 non-cyto 114-132, cytoplasmic C-tail 154-165 — both inter-TM loops luminal/extracellular, both tails cytoplasmic. Matches assumed topology.
  • Phobius mis-labels TM1 as a cleaved signal peptide (1-37); TMHMM recovers it cleanly as a TM helix. NKG7 is not secreted, so TM1 is a genuine membrane anchor — 4-TM count stands.
  • C-terminal YETL (residues 162-165) matches canonical YxxPhi tyrosine sorting motif on the cytoplasmic tail — consistent with granule/lysosomal-membrane targeting.
  • NO ITAM (Yxx[LI]-x6-12-Yxx[LI]) and no functional cytoplasmic ITIM: cytoplasmic segments too short (N-tail 6 aa, TM2-TM3 loop 6 aa, C-tail 12 aa). 7. Tier 0E — Monomer pore scan (the informative negative)

Method. Pore-radius profile along the membrane normal of the NKG7 human and mouse AF monomers, using a HOLE-equivalent axis-tethered probe-sphere sweep (the reference HOLE binary was not installable in-sandbox; the geometric method HOLE itself implements was used at 0.5 Å slices with Bondi vdW radii). Membrane normal defined as the principal axis of the four-TM CA bundle.

  • Human NKG7 monomer: narrowest constriction in the TM span = 0.65 Å (at z=15.9 along membrane normal); TM span 40.5 Å.
  • Mouse NKG7 monomer: narrowest constriction = 0.79 Å (at z=-10.3); TM span 40.3 Å — replicates the human result.
  • Both constrictions (0.65–0.79 Å) fall BELOW the ~1.15 Å radius a single water needs and below the ~0.95–1.0 Å a fully dehydrated Na+ needs; radius stays below the 1.15 Å water threshold across 39% (human, 32/82 slices) / 21% (mouse, 17/81) of the TM span, and below the 1.0 Å dehydrated-Na+ threshold across 17% / 10%.
  • Max radius within TM span only 3.08 Å (human) / 2.52 Å (mouse); wider (>2 Å) values occur only at the membrane-boundary mouths, not through the core.
  • The four-TM bundle packs as a closed helical bundle: NO continuous water- or ion-accessible axial pathway through the monomer.
  • Membrane normal defined objectively as the principal (long) axis of the four-TM CA bundle (res 9–29,61–81,92–112,133–153); pore radius from a HOLE-equivalent axis-tethered probe-sphere sweep at 0.5 Å slices with Bondi vdW radii.
  • Verdict: monomer is occluded — no through-membrane pore — as expected for a lone 4-TM subunit; this is the informative negative that motivates the Tier-1 oligomer search.
  • HOLE binary / hole2 conda package not installable in sandbox (not on allowlisted channels, not on PyPI); used the geometric probe-sphere method HOLE itself implements — qualitative occluded verdict robust to the ±~0.2 Å numeric uncertainty.
Reading: The narrowest constriction is 0.65 Å (human) / 0.79 Å (mouse) — well below the ~1.15 Å a single water needs and the ~1.0 Å a dehydrated Na+ needs — and stays sub-water across 21–39% of the TM span. The four-TM bundle packs closed: no through-membrane pathway in the monomer. This is the expected negative for a lone 4-TM subunit and is precisely why Tier 1 asks whether an oligomer opens a pore at subunit interfaces.

8. Interpretation & gate decision

Gate: ESCALATE to Tier 1. Four of five workstreams recommend escalate; the fifth (transcripts) is permissive. The architectural prerequisite for the channel hypothesis is met — NKG7 is genuinely a claudin/Ca_V-γ tetraspan — so the decisive oligomer/pore test is warranted. But Tier 0 sets a skeptical prior: NKG7's every structural and evolutionary analog is a non-conducting auxiliary subunit or paracellular tetraspan, it lacks any ion-transport or signalling domain, and its monomer is occluded. If Tier 1 finds a confident symmetric oligomer with a continuous hydrated pore, that would be a genuinely surprising positive; if the best oligomer packs shut (as for its Ca_V-γ/claudin relatives), that is a clean result for a scaffold/regulatory role and against a conducting channel.

9. Limitations

  • Static AlphaFold monomers; no membrane, side-chain relaxation, or gating dynamics — a closed monomer need not represent any physiological conducting state.
  • Monomer pore used a HOLE-equivalent geometric method, not the reference HOLE binary (qualitative occluded verdict is robust to the ±~0.2 Å numeric uncertainty; the Tier-1 oligomer re-scan should use the reference HOLE/CHAP on marvin).
  • Foldseek similarity is fold-level (low sequence identity); family membership does not itself imply shared function.
  • GTEx transcript-level quantification covers 5 GENCODE-v26 transcripts, not all 13 Ensembl models.
  • All conclusions are computational; channel function is defined electrophysiologically and requires wet-lab confirmation (patch clamp / bilayer reconstitution / Ca2+-flux imaging).

Proteins with four membrane passes; includes claudins, MP20, PMP-22/EMP, and Ca_V-γ (CACNG) subunits.
Claudin
Tight-junction tetraspan that forms paracellular ion pores between cells (not a transmembrane channel within one membrane).
Ca_V-γ subunit (CACNG) / TARP
Auxiliary, non-conducting tetraspan subunit that regulates Ca2+- and AMPA-type channels; the structural analog cited for NKG7.
